Hi, I am Valentina González

I am a student at Universidad de los Andes, and I would like to share a bit about myself and how I came to choose this career. I have always had an entrepreneurial spirit, largely influenced by my father, who taught me from a young age the importance of having my own business. Because of this, I developed a strong interest in the world of business. He taught me how to negotiate and often took me with him so I could observe how he worked and learn from him. Although at the beginning I felt a bit shy when managing my own small businesses, with the support of my partner I have been able to grow more confident over time. I started by selling squishies when they were trending. I made some sales, but once the trend passed, I stopped. Later, I sold homemade alfajores, and I was always able to sell everything I made. However, preparing them myself required a lot of time and effort. After that, together with my partner, we decided to import women’s jeans from the United States, which is what I am currently focused on. We have been growing our business, expanding beyond jeans to include tops, dresses, and most recently, press-on nails, which I personally design and create. Throughout my academic journey, I have been able to apply more and more of what I learn at university to my business.
